Facility Layout


Overview

Facility Layout, as a core research domain in Industrial Engineering and Operations Research, is fundamentally concerned with the optimal spatial arrangement of physical units within a facility. Due to the NP-hard nature of these problems, a significant portion of research is dedicated to developing sophisticated solution methodologies. This includes creating exact algorithms for smaller instances and, more prevalently, designing advanced metaheuristics like Genetic Algorithms, Simulated Annealing, and Tabu Search to generate high-quality, feasible layouts for large-scale, real-world applications. The field has evolved beyond its traditional manufacturing roots to address the complexities of modern operations. Current research explores dynamic and stochastic layouts that can adapt to changing product mixes and demand uncertainties. It also deeply integrates with other systems, investigating the simultaneous design of layouts and material handling systems, such as Automated Guided Vehicles (AGVs). Furthermore, the scope has expanded to include specialized environments like warehouse storage/retrieval systems, healthcare facilities for improving patient flow, and service operations for enhancing customer experience.

Syllabus

Course Duration: 14 weeks

Course Prerequisites: Operations Research

Course Objectives: - Learn the principles of facility design and layout planning - Understand the relationship between production processes and layout decisions - Explore different types of facility layouts (e.g., process layout, product layout, fixed-position layout) - Analyze the impact of layout decisions on operational efficiency and cost

Assessment Methods: - Homework and assignments: 30% - Midterm exam: 30% - Final exam: 40%
